Slasher

"We stopped checking for monsters under our bed when we realized they were inside us." - Charles Darwin

Slasher films are a horror subgenre that features a masked killer who stalks and murders a group of people, usually by using a blade or sharp tool.

A common theme in these slasher films is to critize activites, most of which are common amoung teenagers, like sex, drugs, and rock n roll.

The sub-genre slasher was considered to have started with Alfred Hitchcock's "Psycho" in 1960.

Some characteristics that are involved in the supernatural horror genre are:
